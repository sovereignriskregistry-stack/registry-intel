In standard B2B shipping contracts, "Force Majeure" is often a catch-all for delays. However, in the context of a potential LNG tanker blockade, this legal mechanism becomes a weaponized defense for logistics providers. Organizations must audit their current Master Service Agreements (MSAs) specifically for clauses regarding "Regional Instability" and "State-Ordered Export Restrictions."

III. Fuel Cost Mitigation: Beyond the Index

When Brent crude sits at $112.78 with an upward trajectory toward $140, traditional fuel surcharges—which usually rely on a trailing 30-day index—fail to protect corporate margins. These indices operate in arrears, leaving the importer exposed to the "delta of volatility."

For firms utilizing private aviation for rapid transit of energy assets or personnel in high-risk zones, integrating your fuel risk is critical. The divergence in pricing between bunker fuel and aviation kerosene is narrowing as the UAE's sovereign policy shifts toward refinery prioritization for internal stability.

We recommend a shift from percentage-based surcharges to flat-rate emergency risk premiums. By decoupling the surcharge from the Brent index, you gain cost predictability at the expense of upside gains. In a $140 oil environment, predictability is the only currency that matters to shareholders.

The UAE’s exit is a signal of "Sovereign Decoupling." Global energy firms must realize that their historical "preferred buyer" status with ADNOC assets may now be subordinated to the UAE’s strategic national security requirements.

Actionable Asset Protection:
