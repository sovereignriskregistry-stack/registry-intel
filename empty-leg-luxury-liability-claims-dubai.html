Unlike standard chartered flights, Empty Legs exist in a quasi-commercial grey area. Liability analysis indicates that many brokers fail to adequately disclose the variance in safety oversight compared to a standard mission-critical flight. Key technical risks include:

  • Vicarious Liability: In the event of an incident, plaintiffs are increasingly piercing the corporate veil to target the beneficial owner of the aircraft, arguing that the pilot's authorization for the "empty" positioning was functionally equivalent to a commercial engagement.
  • Operational Fatigue Variance: Empty legs are often scheduled to maximize asset utilization. Data from our 2026 audits show a 22% higher incidence of pilot fatigue on repositioning flights compared to outbound revenue flights.
  • Insurance Policy "Gaps": Many standard insurance policies contain "territorial exclusions" or "commercial use definitions" that do not strictly cover the positioning phase under specific luxury-tier service agreements.
