1. The Global 7500: Technical Context & Risk Drivers

The Global 7500 represents the pinnacle of ultra-long-range business aviation. However, its sophisticated fly-by-wire architecture and advanced avionics suite have introduced specific technical risks under extreme operational environments. In May 2026, several factors contribute to elevated liability profiles:

  • Avionics Interoperability Issues: Recent reports suggest latent software integration issues during prolonged high-latitude operations.
  • Supply Chain Fragility: Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) parts delays have led to non-standard MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul) interventions, potentially shifting liability from manufacturers to operators.
  • Singapore Jurisdictional Focus: As a primary hub for Asia-Pacific HNW and corporate aviation, Singapore's courts are increasingly acting as the venue of choice for complex cross-border aviation disputes.
