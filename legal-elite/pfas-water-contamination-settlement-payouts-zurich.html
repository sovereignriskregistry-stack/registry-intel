The "PFAS Reckoning" has moved beyond class-action municipal water suits into professional liability and director-and-officer (D&O) spheres. Two landmark precedents define the current liability horizon:

  • State of New Jersey v. Industrial Polymers (2025): The court established that "knowledge of persistence" (bioaccumulation) constitutes constructive intent, effectively triggering the "Known Loss Doctrine" to negate standard policy exclusions.
