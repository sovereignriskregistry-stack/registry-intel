3. Legal Precedents and Liability Frameworks

The transition from traditional "Fault-Based" liability to "Operational Risk" liability is the most significant development in Abu Dhabi civil law for 2026.

  • The "Duty of Supervision" Doctrine: Recent rulings have established that fleet operators are liable not just for the driver's actions, but for the "operational state" of the vehicle, including fatigue management and telematics maintenance.
  • Loss of Future Earnings Calculations: Courts are increasingly accepting aggressive expert testimony regarding "projected economic output" for claimants, leading to significant multipliers in final settlement figures.
  • Vicarious Liability Limitations: Defense strategies are increasingly failing when relying solely on independent contractor status for drivers; the courts are piercing the corporate veil to ensure compensation for victims.
