3. The Legal Frontier: Precedents & Liability Shift

The UAE judicial landscape has evolved significantly. Two primary precedents from 2025-2026 have shifted the burden of proof in "vicarious liability" claims:

  • Case 2025-AD-992 (Al-Jabri v. Trans-Gulf Logistics): The Supreme Court ruled that carrier telemetry data is admissible as primary evidence of driver fatigue. Failure to integrate AI-based fatigue monitoring systems is now categorized as "Gross Negligence" (Article 282 of the Civil Code), rendering standard indemnity caps unenforceable.
  • Case 2026-AD-114 (The Petro-Shield Liability Claim): In the wake of the 41-tanker blockade, courts have expanded the definition of "Foreseeable Harm" for logistics firms operating in hazardous transport corridors. Firms must now carry higher limits of Liability Insurance or face punitive personal injury settlements.
