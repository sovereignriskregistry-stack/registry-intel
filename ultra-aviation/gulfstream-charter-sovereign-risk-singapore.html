The legal landscape has shifted toward the "Doctrine of Sovereign Necessity." Recent rulings indicate that charter operators can no longer rely on standard "Force Majeure" clauses to shield against government-directed groundings. Key legal considerations include:

  • Corporate Veil Piercing: We have observed a trend in the Singapore High Court where beneficial owners are being named in secondary litigation following failed charter deliveries due to sovereign interference.
