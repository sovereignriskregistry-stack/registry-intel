Regulatory Liability and Risk Exposure

The primary danger in private aviation is the “veil of compliance.” If a charter operator is found to be non-compliant—or worse, operating illegally—the end-user (the passenger or the corporation) can be held vicariously liable for safety violations and tax delinquency. Our forensics team scrutinizes:

  • Operational Control: Determining if the flight is being operated under the correct FAA certificate.
  • Maintenance Logs: Analyzing the provenance of parts and the validity of airworthiness directives (ADs) to ensure your safety isn't compromised by deferred maintenance.
  • Contractual Integrity: Reviewing the "Dry Lease" vs. "Wet Lease" agreements to ensure your flight department is not inadvertently assuming the role of an illegal commercial operator.
