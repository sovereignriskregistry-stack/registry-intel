Expert Analysis

Compliance forensics focuses on three critical pillars:

  • Regulatory Liability: Operators in Hong Kong must adhere to stringent CAD safety protocols. Liability often shifts to the charterer if the operator is found to be utilizing "grey market" aircraft—private jets registered under shell companies to bypass safety oversight. Our forensic approach verifies the Air Operator Certificate (AOC) and ensures that the flight crew’s duty hours are not being masked by manipulated digital logs.
  • Cost Transparency and Forensic Auditing: Hidden costs are pervasive in jet chartering, particularly concerning landing fees, ground handling, and repositioning costs. Forensic audits track "phantom charges" often buried in invoices, ensuring that the charterer is not absorbing the costs of the operator's commercial inefficiencies or unauthorized sub-chartering.
  • Risk Mitigation: We evaluate the legal enforceability of insurance riders. Many HNWIs find their insurance invalidated because the charter operator failed to disclose specific flight paths or cargo manifests that fall outside the standard scope of coverage.
