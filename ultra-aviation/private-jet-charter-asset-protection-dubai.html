Strategic Consultation Checklist: Protecting Your Aviation Interests

Before entering or renewing a charter arrangement, stakeholders must conduct a comprehensive due diligence audit. The following checklist identifies the essential pillars of asset protection:

  • Jurisdictional Alignment: Is your charter contract governed by DIFC Law or English Common Law? Ensure the governing jurisdiction aligns with your family office’s existing asset protection trust structure.
  • Indemnity Verification: Does the operator’s insurance coverage specifically include "Additional Insured" status for the charterer? If not, the charterer remains exposed to subrogation by the operator’s insurers in the event of a total loss.
  • AOC & Sub-Charter Transparency: Does the charter agreement explicitly prohibit "sub-chartering" to third-party aircraft without written consent? Asset protection is compromised if the aircraft boarding the client is not part of the operator’s primary safety management system (SMS).
  • Cyber-Aviation Security: With the rise of spoofing and cyber-intelligence targeting high-net-worth travel logs, verify that your operator’s flight dispatch systems comply with current cybersecurity standards for PII (Personally Identifiable Information) protection.
  • Regulatory Resilience: Review the operator’s current status with the GCAA. Ensure all maintenance logs for the specific tail numbers under contract are documented and independently auditable by your personal aviation consultant.
  • Emergency Extraction Protocols: Does the charter agreement include provisions for rapid asset deployment in the event of regional instability? In 2026, the most effective asset protection is the ability to move human and capital assets out of a volatile region within a 6-hour window.
