Strategic Consultation Checklist: Pre-Flight and Post-Incident Governance

The following checklist is designed for Corporate Counsel, Risk Managers, and Family Office Executives to assess their posture regarding charter agreements.

Pre-Charter Due Diligence

  • Operator Audit: Verify the SMS certification of the operator. Is it compliant with IS-BAO (International Standard for Business Aircraft Operations) Stage 3?
  • Liability Limitation Review: Confirm that the "Certificate of Insurance" (COI) lists your organization as an "Additional Insured" with a primary and non-contributory waiver of subrogation.
  • Geopolitical Risk Clause: Ensure the charter agreement includes a force majeure carve-out that protects against sudden regulatory closures of UAE airspace due to regional instability.

Immediate Post-Incident Protocols

  • Evidence Preservation (The "Golden Hour"): Notify the operator in writing to immediately preserve flight data recorder (FDR) and cockpit voice recorder (CVR) data. In the UAE jurisdiction, digital evidence is paramount.
  • Appoint Independent Counsel: Do not rely exclusively on the operator’s insurance counsel, whose primary duty is the preservation of the operator’s balance sheet, not your organization’s risk profile.
  • Media Containment: In the hyper-connected Abu Dhabi business ecosystem, information leaks at an accelerated rate. Engage a specialized crisis communications firm before the first press inquiry hits.
  • GCAA Liaison: Establish a clear communication line with the Air Accident Investigation Sector (AAIS). Ensure all statements are vetted to prevent "Admission of Liability" traps in potential future civil litigation.
