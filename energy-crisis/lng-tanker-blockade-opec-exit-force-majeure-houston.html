3. The 'Force Majeure' Minefield

In the wake of supply disruptions, we anticipate a deluge of Force Majeure (FM) claims. As a global risk consultant, I must emphasize: Force Majeure is not a 'get out of jail free' card.

Recent jurisprudence in the Houston energy hub indicates that courts are becoming increasingly skeptical of FM claims predicated on market price fluctuations. To successfully trigger an FM clause during the current crisis, counsel must prove that:

  1. The specific blockade or political event was unforeseeable at the time of contract execution.
  2. The event rendered performance impossible, not merely commercially impracticable or unprofitable.
  3. The company took all reasonable steps to mitigate the impact, including sourcing alternate supply at higher cost.

Energy firms must conduct immediate audit of all Master Sales and Purchase Agreements (MSPA). If your FM clause does not explicitly define 'geopolitical supply chain obstruction' as a trigger, you are currently carrying unhedged exposure.
